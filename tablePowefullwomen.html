Savitribai Phule

Birth: Savitribai was born on January 3, 1831, in Naigaon village, which is now in Maharashtra state, India. Family Background: She belonged to the Mali community, a socially backward group at the time, where education for women was not common. Marriage: At the young age of nine, Savitribai was married to Jyotirao Phule, who was thirteen years old and a prominent social reformer. Education: Despite the societal norms, Jyotirao Phule supported Savitribai's education, and she became one of the first women in India to receive formal education.

Pioneer of Women's Education: She and her husband established the first Indian-run girls' school in Pune in 1848, defying societal norms that discouraged women's education. Social Reformer: Savitribai worked to abolish discrimination based on caste and gender, advocating for the rights of marginalized communities, including Dalits and women. Advocate for Widow Rights: She provided shelter and support for widows, who were often ostracized and humiliated in that era.

She strived to abolish discrimination and unfair treatment of people based on caste and gender. She and her husband were pioneers of women's education in India. They started their first school for girls in 1848 in Pune at Tatyasaheb Bhide's residence or Bhidewada. Bust of Savitribai Phule. The woman who helped set up the first school for girls in India. Savitribai Phule was a trailblazer in providing education for girls and for ostracized portions of society. She became the first female teacher in India (1848) and opened a school for girls with her husband, Jyotirao Phule.

She was the daughter of S. Swaminathan, a lawyer, and A.V. Ammukutty, a social worker and independence activies Lakshmi was inspired by her mother's progressive views and activism.

Captain Lakshmi Sahgal, a freedom fighter, medical practitioner, and social activist, significantly contributed to society by serving in the Indian National Army (INA), founding the All India Democratic Women's Association (AIDWA), and advocating for women's rights and social justice. Sahgal was one of the founding members of the All India Democratic Women's Association in 1981 and led many of its campaigns. She worked towards restoring peace and harmony in Kanpur following the anti-Sikh riots of 1984.

Lakshmi married Prem Kumar Sahgal in March 1947 in Lahore. After their marriage, they settled in Kanpur, where she continued with her medical practice and aided the refugees who were arriving in large numbers following the Partition of India. They had two daughters: Subhashini Ali and Anisa Puri. Subhashini is a prominent communist politician and labour activist. According to Ali, Lakshmi was an atheist.
